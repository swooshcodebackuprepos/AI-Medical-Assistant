1) Most likely rhythm: Atrial fibrillation

2) Supporting features:
- Irregularly irregular ventricular rhythm (no consistent R-R intervals)
- Absence of distinct, consistent P waves before QRS complexes
- Presence of fibrillatory baseline oscillations between QRS complexes
- Normal QRS width (narrow complex, no evidence of bundle branch block)
- Variable ventricular rate (appears moderately controlled rate)

3) Key differentials and how to distinguish:
- Atrial flutter with variable block: flutter waves (sawtooth pattern), often at a regular atrial rate around 250-350 bpm, distinguish by looking for consistent flutter waves especially in inferior leads
- Multifocal atrial tachycardia (MAT): presence of at least 3 distinct P wave morphologies; irregular rhythm but identifiable P waves, distinguish by examining multiple different P wave shapes
- Sinus arrhythmia: presence of consistent P waves with normal morphology preceding each QRS complex; rhythm irregular but usually related to respiratory cycle, distinguish by P wave pattern and regular PR intervals
