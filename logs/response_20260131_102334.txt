1) Most likely rhythm: Sinus rhythm

2) Supporting features:
- Rate: Approximately 70-80 bpm (normal range)
- Regularity: Regular rhythm, consistent R-R intervals
- P waves: Present before each QRS complex, upright morphology
- P/QRS ratio: 1:1 conduction (each P wave followed by a QRS)
- PR interval: Normal duration (approximately 0.12 to 0.20 seconds)
- QRS width: Normal narrow complexes (<0.12 seconds)

3) Key differentials and distinguishing features:
- Sinus tachycardia: Faster rate (>100 bpm) but otherwise similar EKG features; distinguished by increased rate.
- Atrial flutter with 2:1 block: Regular rhythm but P waves replaced by flutter ("sawtooth") waves and often no distinct P before each QRS; can distinguish by characteristic flutter waves and atrial rate.
- Junctional rhythm: Absent or inverted P waves and usually slower rate; distinguish by P wave morphology and timing.
